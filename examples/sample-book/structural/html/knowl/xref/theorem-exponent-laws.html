        <p>In a group, the usual laws of exponents hold; that is, for all <m>g, h \in G</m>,<ol>
            <li><p><m>g^mg^n = g^{m+n}</m> for all <m>m, n \in {\mathbb Z}</m>;</p></li>
            <li><p><m>(g^m)^n = g^{mn}</m> for all <m>m, n \in {\mathbb Z}</m>;</p></li>
            <li><p><m>(gh)^n = (h^{-1}g^{-1})^{-n}</m> for all <m>n \in {\mathbb Z}</m>. Furthermore, if <m>G</m> is abelian, then <m>(gh)^n = g^nh^n</m>.</p></li>
        </ol></p>
    </statement>
</theorem>
