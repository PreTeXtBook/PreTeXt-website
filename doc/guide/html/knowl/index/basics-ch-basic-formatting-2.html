By a markup language, we mean that the syntax describes the structure of the document and not the presentation of the document. Thus, PreTeXt does not provide, for instance, a way to make text bold or italic or in a larger font. If an author seeks a specific type of local typesetting, then they need to pause and think about the reason for that typesetting. Is the reason to emphasize a word or phrase? Is the reason to alert the reader to a common mistake? Is it to designate that a word is a new term being defined by the author? There are ways to mark up such structural ideas in PreTeXt, and authors should conscientiously ensure that they use this markup.
